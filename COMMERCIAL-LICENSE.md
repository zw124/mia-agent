# Commercial Licensing

Mia is offered under a dual-license model:

- Community Edition: `AGPL-3.0-only`
- Commercial Edition: available under a separate commercial agreement

## When a commercial license is required

You should contact the maintainers for a commercial license if you want to:

- embed Mia into a proprietary product or service;
- modify and deploy Mia without making the full corresponding source available under the AGPL;
- offer Mia as part of a paid hosted product where AGPL obligations are not acceptable to your business;
- distribute proprietary extensions that form a single combined work with AGPL-covered code.

## What the commercial license can cover

A commercial agreement may include:

- proprietary distribution rights;
- private deployment rights;
- warranty, indemnity, or commercial support terms;
- negotiated terms for internal forks and extensions.

## Contact

Commercial licensing inquiries should be sent to the project owner through the repository contact channels or a private business contact designated by the maintainer.

## Relationship to the open source license

This document does not replace the open source license for the community edition. Unless you have a signed commercial agreement, your use of this repository is governed by `AGPL-3.0-only`.
